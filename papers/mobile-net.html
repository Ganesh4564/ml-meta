MobileNets: Efficient CNNs for Mobile Vision

Introduction

MobileNets are designed to be fast and lightweight for deployment on mobile and embedded devices. They introduce depthwise separable convolutions to significantly reduce computation and parameters.

Depthwise Separable Convolutions

Instead of performing a single heavy convolution operation, MobileNet breaks it into two steps:

  • Depthwise convolution: Applies one filter per input channel.
  • Pointwise convolution: Uses 1×1 convolutions to combine the output.

Computation comparison:

$$ \text{Standard cost} = D_K \cdot D_K \cdot M \cdot N \cdot D_F \cdot D_F $$ $$ \text{Depthwise + Pointwise} = D_K \cdot D_K \cdot M \cdot D_F \cdot D_F + M \cdot N \cdot D_F \cdot D_F $$

Hyperparameters

  • Width Multiplier (α): Shrinks the number of channels (model size).
  • Resolution Multiplier (ρ): Scales the input image resolution (speed vs accuracy).

Performance

  • Accuracy: 70.6% (top-1 on ImageNet)
  • Mult-Adds: 569 million
  • Parameters: 4.2 million

Applications

MobileNets are widely used for real-time applications, including:

  • Mobile object detection
  • Facial recognition
  • Fine-grained classification
  • Geo-localization (e.g., Google’s PlaNet)
